## Figure design: “Reporting of standard vs. fairness metrics in histopathology AI studies (n = 78)”

### Layout
Two horizontally aligned bar charts (Panel A and Panel B) sharing the same x‑axis scale (0–100 %) and a common y‑axis label “Metric”. The figure should be compact and colour‑coded: e.g., teal for standard metrics, orange for fairness metrics.

**Panel A – Standard performance metrics**  
Horizontal bars for the six metrics you reported with percentages and 95% confidence intervals:

| Metric | % (95% CI) |
|--------|-------------|
| Accuracy | 85.9 (76.5–92.0) |
| AUROC | 70.5 (59.7–79.5) |
| F1 | 42.3 (32.0–53.2) |
| Sensitivity / specificity | 39.7 (29.7–50.8) |
| Balanced accuracy | 17.9 (11.1–28.0) |

**Panel B – Fairness reporting**  
A single, prominent bar for the overall prevalence of **any fairness metric** (19.2%, CI 12.0–29.3%).  
Optionally, add a second, smaller bar for **worst‑group accuracy** (7.7%, CI 3.6–16.0%) to underscore that even the most basic equity check is rarely done.  
A dashed vertical line at 80.8% with the annotation “80.8% of studies reported no fairness metric” provides the crucial counter‑point.

If you prefer to show the fairness‑metric *families* that appear in Table 1, you could break out Panel B into a small inset or a second row, but the stark Panel B‑only version is far more powerful for the main paper. A detailed family breakdown can remain in the supplement.
