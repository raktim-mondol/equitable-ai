\section{Eight-Category Bias Taxonomy: Full Definitions and Exemplar Findings}
\label{app:S2}

Table~\ref{tab:bias_taxonomy} provides the complete working definitions
and exemplar empirical findings for the eight bias categories identified
across the 78 included studies (Figure~\ref{fig:bias_taxonomy} in the
main text). Study counts are non-exclusive: individual studies
typically report multiple co-occurring bias sources.

\begin{table}[htbp]
  \centering
  \caption{Eight-category bias taxonomy derived from the 78~included
           histopathology fairness studies. Study counts are
           non-exclusive: individual studies typically report multiple
           co-occurring bias sources (see
           Figure~\ref{fig:bias_taxonomy}).}
  \label{tab:bias_taxonomy}
  \small
  \begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{@{}p{2.3cm} X X@{}}
    \toprule
    \textbf{Category ($n$)} & \textbf{Working definition} &
      \textbf{Exemplar finding} \\
    \midrule
    Demographic (5) &
      Performance varies by race, ethnicity, ancestry, sex, or age. &
      White--Black AUROC gap 3--16\% across 20 cancer types;
      race predictable from skin histology (AUROC~0.70)~\citep{vaidya2024demographic,
      chen2025predicting, lin2025contrastive, huang2025knowledge}. \\
    \addlinespace[2pt]
    Institutional / batch (7) &
      Site, scanner, or staining protocol signatures encoded by
      the model. &
      Site classification AUROC~$>$0.96 on TCGA; foundation-model
      features probe TSS at $>$90\%~\citep{howard2021site,
      dehkharghanian2023biased, komen2024batch, kheiri2025investigation}. \\
    \addlinespace[2pt]
    Selection / sampling (8) &
      Non-representative cohorts (single-centre, geographic skew,
      convenience sampling). &
      $\sim$33/78 studies anchored on TCGA; $>$70\% White cohorts
      yield lower non-White accuracy~\citep{soltan2024challenges,
      celi2022sources, musa2026cross, tasci2022bias}. \\
    \addlinespace[2pt]
    Technical / domain-shift (8) &
      Scanner, stain, fixation, or preprocessing variation causing
      distribution shift. &
      Within-batch AUROC 0.74--0.96 collapses to 0.52--0.61 cross-batch;
      stain normalisation fails~\citep{lin2025stain, komen2024batch,
      aubreville2022mitosis, roschewitz2023automatic}. \\
    \addlinespace[2pt]
    Class / label (7) &
      Class imbalance, label noise, inter-observer variability,
      annotation shortcuts. &
      Long-tail TCGA sub-types; grayscale conversion drops centre
      accuracy by 15\%~\citep{tasci2022bias, hagele2020resolving,
      kheiri2025investigation, vanbooven2025mitigating}. \\
    \addlinespace[2pt]
    Algorithmic (5) &
      Bias introduced by architecture, MIL attention, objective, or
      model-selection choices. &
      Standard MIL attention dilutes salient instances; model-selection
      rule alters worst-group AUROC~\citep{ling2024agent,
      campanella2019clinical, zong2023medfair, cecconi2024fairness}. \\
    \addlinespace[2pt]
    Confounding (6) &
      Non-causal site--demographic, disease--demographic, or co-slide
      associations exploited as shortcuts. Disease prevalence differences
      across groups create spurious correlations. &
      Ancestry AUROC 0.798$\to$0.507 under preserved-site CV;
      MI(label, centre)~$=1.47$ (Kheiri et al.); disease--demographic
      confounding (Chen et al.)~\citep{howard2021site, chen2025predicting,
      kheiri2025investigation}. \\
    \addlinespace[2pt]
    Representation (7) &
      Under-coverage of populations, skin tones, cancer sub-types, or
      geographies in training data. &
      $>$70\% White cohorts yield lower non-White accuracy;
      rare sub-types undertrained~\citep{soltan2024challenges,
      vaidya2024demographic, ktena2024generative, tasci2022bias}. \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabularx}
\end{table}
